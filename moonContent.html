The Moon

The Moon is Earth’s only natural satellite and has a profound impact on our planet.

It influences tides, stabilizes Earth’s rotation, and has inspired humans for centuries.

The Moon’s surface is marked with craters, mountains, and vast plains of volcanic rock.

It takes about 27.3 days to orbit Earth and the same time to rotate once on its axis.

This synchronous rotation is why we always see the same face of the Moon from Earth.

Scientists believe the Moon formed about 4.5 billion years ago after a giant impact.

It has no atmosphere, weather, or liquid water, but ice exists in shadowed craters.

The Moon has been visited by astronauts during NASA’s Apollo missions between 1969 and 1972.

Future missions aim to establish a sustainable human presence on the Moon.

It continues to fascinate astronomers, explorers, and dreamers across the world.
