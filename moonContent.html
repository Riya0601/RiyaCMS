The Moon

The Moon is Earth’s only natural satellite. It influences tides, stabilizes Earth’s rotation, and has been the focus of exploration for centuries.

Its surface is covered with craters, plains, and mountains formed by impacts and volcanic activity.

Despite appearing bright, the Moon reflects only a small percentage of sunlight.

Scientists believe the Moon formed around 4.5 billion years ago from debris after a giant impact with Earth.

Future missions aim to establish permanent bases for exploration and research.
