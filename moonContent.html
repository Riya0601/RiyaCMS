The Moon

The Moon is Earth's only natural satellite and has inspired humanity for centuries. It controls the tides and lights our night skies.

Its surface is pockmarked with craters and vast basalt plains called maria, formed by ancient lava flows.

Humans first visited the Moon in 1969 with Apollo 11; we continue to plan future missions and bases.

The Moon's phases and eclipses have guided calendars, navigation, and culture across the world.
