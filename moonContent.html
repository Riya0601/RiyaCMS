About the Moon

The Moon is Earth's only natural satellite, orbiting at an average distance of 384,400 km.

It influences ocean tides due to its gravitational pull.

The Moon is about one-fourth the size of Earth.

It has no atmosphere, which makes its surface vulnerable to meteoroid impacts.

The first human landing on the Moon was Apollo 11 in 1969.

Its surface is covered with craters, mountains, and plains called maria.

The Moon completes one orbit around Earth in about 27.3 days.

It shows phases because of the changing angles of sunlight.

Lunar eclipses occur when Earth blocks sunlight from reaching the Moon.

The Moon is slowly drifting away from Earth by about 3.8 cm per year.

It has been a source of inspiration for myths, poems, and art across cultures.

Future missions aim to establish human bases on the Moon.
