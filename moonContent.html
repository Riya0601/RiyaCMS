The Beautiful Moon

The Moon is Earth’s only natural satellite and the fifth largest in the solar system.

It is about 1/4th the size of Earth and has a diameter of 3,474 km.

The Moon plays a crucial role in stabilizing Earth’s tilt and climate.

It influences ocean tides with its gravitational pull.

The surface is covered with craters, mountains, and plains called maria.

The Moon has no atmosphere, which makes temperatures extreme.

Humans first landed on the Moon in 1969 during the Apollo 11 mission.

It has a synchronous rotation, always showing the same face to Earth.

The Moon goes through phases as it orbits Earth every 29.5 days.

Future missions plan to establish lunar bases for research.
